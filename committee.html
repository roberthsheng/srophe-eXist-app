Purpose & Mission

Publicly available since 2018, ArchitecturaSinica is the first open-access digital research tool focused on the architecture of traditional China (primarily the Medieval and Early Modern periods; ca. 3rd-17th centuries CE).

The mission of AS is to advance international teaching of and research into the globally significant architectural traditions of China. Our web portal has two primary divisions: a bilingual (Chinese/English) thesaurus of Architectural Terminology developed by the ATTCAT Project (more below), and a Dynamic Site Archive of important works of early historic Chinese architecture. DSA entries include links to terminological annotations and also include geolocation, historical descriptions, photographs, and line drawings of historic sites. The bibliography of the sources we use, including pages documenting contributions of student and faculty collaborators, is also linked to ATTCAT and DSA pages.

In combining these facets, ArchitecturaSinica provides a means to simultaneously investigate historic structures and the technical terminology used to describe them. Through the linked bibliography, AS is also designed to enhance discovery of the wealth of new scholarship being produced globally in this exciting area of research.

The ATTCAT Project

The ATTCAT project was established to develop the first digital thesaurus of Chinese architecture terminology in the English-speaking world. Beginning in 2017, our mission is to unlock the meanings of technical terms used by builders, craftsmen, and officials through both textual and visual examples. Initiated by Chen Wei 陳薇, Zhuge Jing 諸葛净and Tracy Miller 梅晨曦, our annotations have been available through the AS web portal since 2018, with abbreviated entries available through the Getty Art and Architecture Thesaurus. Longer AS entries include Illustrations, historical examples, and a bibliography of modern and premodern sources included in order to facilitate and inspire future research. Annual ATTCAT meetings are led by Professors Chen Wei, Zhuge Jing, Zuo Lala 左拉拉, and Tracy Miller and are generously sponsored by Southeast University School of Architecture in Nanjing 南京東南大學建築學院 and, since 2021, NYU Shanghai.
